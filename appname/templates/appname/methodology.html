CARBOMICA · Scientific basis

Methodology, emission factors & assumptions

CARBOMICA is built on peer-reviewed and internationally standardised data. Every quantity you enter (kWh, litres, kg, km, units) is converted to tonnes of CO₂-equivalent at the moment it is displayed or optimised, using the factors documented below. Grid electricity uses a country-specific factor (IEA); all other categories use GHG Protocol, UK DEFRA and IPCC AR6 factors. This page is public so it can be cited directly in reports and grant submissions.

Optimisation algorithm — three scenarios

For a facility with attached interventions, each intervention i has a cost cᵢ (implementation + maintenance) and a modelled annual reduction rᵢ = pᵢ × Bₖ, where pᵢ is the intervention's reduction fraction and Bₖ is the converted tCO₂e baseline of the emission category it targets (not the facility total — so Solar PV reduces only the electricity slice).

1 · Full coverage

Apply every attached intervention, ignoring the constraint. Gives the theoretical maximum reduction and total cost.

2 · Constrained, cheapest-first

Sort interventions by ascending cost; add until the budget is exhausted (budget mode) or the reduction target is met (target mode).

3 · Optimised (recommended)

Greedy knapsack: sort by cost-effectiveness rᵢ / cᵢ (tCO₂e per USD), descending; add until the constraint binds. Zero-cost actions rank first.

The greedy knapsack is a transparent, deterministic heuristic — the same inputs always yield the same ranking, which matters for reproducibility and auditability. It is near-optimal for the divisible-budget case typical of facility planning; it does not claim the exact integer-knapsack optimum.

Assumptions & limitations

Rigorous tools state their boundaries. CARBOMICA's results are decision-support estimates, and rest on these assumptions:

  • Linear, independent reductions. Each intervention's reduction is applied to its category baseline independently; the tool does not model interactions (e.g. LED lighting lowering the load a solar array must cover). For small numbers of interventions this is a minor approximation; stacking many that target the same category can overstate combined reduction (capped at 100% of that category).
  • Static grid factors. Country electricity factors are point-in-time (IEA 2022) and do not project grid decarbonisation over the investment horizon.
  • Annualised, steady-state costs. Implementation and maintenance costs are treated as known annual figures; the tool does not model financing, inflation, or staged roll-out beyond NPV discounting.
  • Default costs are regional estimates. Library defaults are LMIC reference values (HIGH Horizons D3.7). Site-specific costs should be entered for any figure used in a real funding decision — rows using defaults are badged default / placeholder.
  • Activity-based accounting. Emissions are estimated from activity data (kWh, litres, kg) × published factors, per the GHG Protocol — not from direct measurement or continuous monitoring.

How conversion happens

You enter raw activity data — e.g. 500000 kWh of grid electricity. CARBOMICA stores the raw value and converts on the fly: tCO₂e = quantity × factor, with the factor chosen by your facility's country for electricity and by category for everything else. The dashboard, facility profiles, and optimiser all run on these converted values.

Intervention impacts are modelled as a percentage reduction applied to the relevant emission category's converted baseline — e.g. Solar PV (70%) applies to your grid-electricity tCO₂e, not to your total.

Factors are reviewed against their sources (IEA, DEFRA/GHG Protocol, IPCC AR6) when those bodies publish updates — values on this page always reflect what the tool is currently using.
